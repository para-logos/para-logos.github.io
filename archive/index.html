:: About ::

Para-Institute for Theoretical Action Research (PITAR) explores the paradox of theory and action at the edge of institutional intelligibility.

* Theory simulates action. Action impersonates theory. *

Welcome to PITAR

The Para-Institute for Theoretical Action Research (PITAR) is an experimental platform dedicated to exploring the threshold between theory and practice, institution and escape, presence and ghost. Inspired by action research yet fundamentally subversive of it, PITAR questions the structures that determine how knowledge is produced, enacted, and legitimized.

Our Premise

At PITAR, we believe that action does not always require intervention, and theory does not always live in abstraction. Instead, we focus on the recursive process in which action produces theory, and theory suspends action. This paradox is not a problem to be solved, but a methodology to be inhabited.

What We Do

  • Performative simulations of research-based interventions
  • Experimental approaches to meta-action methodologies
  • Construction of fictional institutions, researchers, and artifacts
  • Disassembly and repurposing of theoretical apparatuses
  • Production of texts and practices that resemble research—but aren’t (or are)

Who This Is For

PITAR is a space for scholars, artists, thinkers, and experimental practitioners who wish to:

  • Blur the line between theory and practice
  • Engage in research that critiques its own form
  • Pursue existential legitimacy rather than institutional recognition
  • Write what must be written, even if unread

PITAR offers no conclusions—only the possibility that every question can be enacted.

:: Academic Programs ::

  • The Institute offers innovative educational pathways that challenge traditional academic boundaries:
    • Degree Programs:
      • Free-Associate Degree: This pioneering program empowers students to establish their own fields of study not formally recognized by conventional universities. By achieving specified outcomes in their self-designed majors, students can receive academic recognition for exploring uncharted intellectual territories. This approach is ideal for those interested in emergent fields that exist between established disciplines or in cultural phenomena that deserve scholarly attention but have not yet been formalized.
      • Dissociate Degree: This unique program celebrates the pataphysical tradition of creative disruption through academic mimicry. Students develop plausible yet deliberately unconventional theoretical frameworks, engaging in playful philosophy that challenges established systems of thought. This degree recognizes the value of constructive confusion as a tool for intellectual growth and disciplinary innovation.
